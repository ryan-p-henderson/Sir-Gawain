Research Question

The story of Sir Gawain and the Green Knight has influenced wrtiers of fiction, particularly the bob and wheel stanzas, five-line groups at the end of longer sections reacting to the events which have just taken place. has been printed and told over and over again since it was first written in the late 14th century, little knowledge exists regarding its author, a man known only as the Pearl Poet. Although he remains a mysterious figure, it seems as though the Poet shifts voice during segments like the bob and wheel, and he certainly takes on a breadth of characters. Thus, during our initial introduction to XML and other programming languages and subsequent project search, we asked: could computing various cultural references and where they occur in the text give any insight into the authorial intent of the Pearl Poet himself?

About the Text

We began our research using a TEI encoded XML version of Gawain downloaded from the University of Oxford Text Archive (link: http://www.ota.ox.ac.uk/text/3306), with a translation done by J.R.R. Tolkien and E.V. Gordon. This translated version kept to the Middle English of the type, and maintained the use of symbols signifying letter combinations. Ryan spearheaded the markup process, due to his knowledge and experience with Middle English and the story of Gawain in general.
